There once was a city called Atlantis. A group of young explorers stumbled upon it one day. There was something about it that stood out. Something about it that sparkled and glowed. Something that inspired a spirit of wonder. So, they climbed their way into the forest, deep and dark. They found trees that were distorted into magical figures and water that shimmered in the light. They drank it and felt the magic coursing through their veins.

And so they brought their fellow man here. Here, where the magic lived. And those explorers became the leaders. The children who drank from the Hallow. The ones who had magic in their veins. And they used that magic to take care of their people. To keep sickness and harm at arm's length. To keep food growing and water clean. To make sure everyone was thriving and happiness was bountiful. They built columns and churches and homes. Libraries and medical facilities. All was well...

But the magic came with a price. You had to filter it through a piece of Atlantis. You couldn't take it unto yourself directly. Or the magic would find it's way to your brain and make you do bad things. Unspeakable things. Things that weren't of the people or of the light. Things that were of the darkness. And so the children did just that. They took pieces of Atlantis to protect them from the magic. And nothing bad happened. Though they always felt the darkness looming behind them.
